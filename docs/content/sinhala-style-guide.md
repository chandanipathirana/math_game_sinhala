# Sinhala Content Style Guide

## Goals

- Keep every child-facing sentence short.
- Prefer spoken Sinhala over formal textbook Sinhala.
- Use familiar objects such as apples, balls, stars, books, and mangoes.
- Avoid long instructions and stacked clauses.

## Writing Rules

- Prefer 4-10 word prompts when possible.
- Use one action per sentence.
- Avoid abstract nouns and technical math terms for Grade 1.
- Keep praise warm and brief.
- When a child is wrong, avoid blame and encourage the next attempt.

## Example Tone

- Good: `හොඳයි!`
- Good: `ආයෙත් බලමු.`
- Avoid: long corrective paragraphs
- Avoid: exam-style formal instructions

## Audio Rules

- Speak slowly.
- Pause naturally around numbers.
- Use consistent pronunciation for operations and quantities.
